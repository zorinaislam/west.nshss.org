Microsoft’s earnings for Q1 2024 showed strong performance, particularly due to significant growth in its Intelligent Cloud segment, which includes Azure and other cloud services. This segment’s revenue was reported at $24.3 billion, marking a 19.4% increase year over year. Specifically, Azure and other cloud services experienced a growth of 29%. Additionally, Microsoft Cloud, which encompasses Azure, Office 365 Commercial, and other cloud properties, also showed robust performance with revenue of $31.8 billion, up 24% year over year.<\/p>\n

The earnings per share for Microsoft in Q1 2024 were reported at $2.99 on revenue of $56.5 billion, exceeding analyst estimates. This strong start to the fiscal year was attributed to consistent execution by Microsoft’s sales teams and partners. Furthermore, the introduction of AI products like M365 Copilot has been identified as a significant growth catalyst for the company.<\/p>\n

While Microsoft did not raise its outlook for FY2024, possibly due to economic uncertainties and the impact of the Activision acquisition on margins, the overall performance, especially in the cloud segment, indicates a solid position.
